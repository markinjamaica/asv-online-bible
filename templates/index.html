Question: What makes the American Standard Version different from most translations of the Bible?

Answer: It restores God's name to its rightful place in the scriptures.

From the American Revision Committee...

The change first recommended in the Appendix - that which substitutes "Jehovah" for "LORD" and "GOD" - is one which will be unwelcome to many...

But the American Revisers, after a careful consideration were brought to the unanimous conviction that a Jewish superstition, which regarded the Divine Name as too sacred to be uttered, ought no longer to dominate in the English or any other version of the Old Testament...

This Memorial Name...designates God as the personal God, as the covenant God, the God of revelation, the Deliverer, the Friend of his people...the ever living Helper of those who are in trouble. This personal name, with its wealth of sacred associations, is now restored to the place in the sacred text to which it has an unquestionable claim.
